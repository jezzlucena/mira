## 5. Product Pillars

### The "All-Habits" Approach
Most apps bifurcate habits into "Do" and "Don't." We view habits as a spectrum. Users can track:
* **Biological needs:** Sleep, water, medication.
* **Productivity:** Reading news, coding, cooking.
* **Vices/Coping Mechanisms:** Smoking, nail-biting, gambling, spending.

### The Sentiment Engine
The core differentiator of Mira is the correlation engine.
* **Input:** User logs "Bite Nails" + Mood `2/6` (Anxious).
* **Output:** Analytics show a heat map correlating low mood scores with specific habit triggers.
* **Goal:** Move from reactive behavior to conscious choice.

### Privacy First
Because we allow the tracking of sensitive subjects (substance use, mental health struggles), user privacy is paramount.
* End-to-End Encryption options.
* Local-first data storage preference.
* Non-judgmental export (User owns their data).

## 7. Target Audience

1.  **The Self-Optimizer:** People looking to fine-tune sleep, diet, and productivity.
2.  **The Harm Reducer:** Individuals managing addiction or compulsive behaviors who want to track frequency without the pressure of "cold turkey" quitting.
3.  **The Neurodivergent User:** People with ADHD or Autism who struggle with traditional gamified trackers that demand perfection.
4.  **The Mental Health Advocate:** Users using data to communicate better with therapists or medical professionals.

---

## 8. Success Metrics

* **Retention over Streaks:** We measure success by how long a user keeps *logging*, not by how long they maintain a "perfect" streak.
* **Insight Generation:** Can the user identify a trigger for a bad habit within 30 days of use?
* **Sentiment Shift:** Do users report a stabilization in mood (closer to 3-4 on the scale) regarding their tracked habits over time?
